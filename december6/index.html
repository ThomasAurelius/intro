Self Teaching Web Development, for a fraction of the cost of a Boot Camp.

I was looking over half a dozen options for learning and updating my Web Development skills. None of them seemed to hit all my requirements. So, I decided to self-teach, using whatever online resources I can access for free, or nominal fees. This site is my evolving test playground, but also a way to document my journey, so I can share this experience. Maybe it helps someone who comes after me decide how they approach their own journey.

Resource Reviews

As of December 1st, I am 14 working days in now. For the below, I plan to update my current usage and opinions on what I interact with.

CodecademyPro

  • Current Opinion: My current main learning platform. Good content in the Pro subscription.
  • Value: Still high. As of now, I plan to keep it through the end of Jan.
  • Competition: LinkedIn Learning. LIL offers all of its lessons via video. They do have supplemental files and excercises you can follow along with. But, for me, who has such a wide array of access points, it's difficult to keep current on the exercise every where I go. But, the videos are very high quality, with knowledgable experts that are excellent presenters. I listen to the videos, more than I watch and follow along. However, this page, is modified from on of their supplemental files in their CSS courses.
  • Major Pros: The in-browser follow along environment.
  • Major Cons: Nothing major, however, sometimes the validation of the in-browser developer, is finicky. Sometimes the lesson has the input wrong, or the validation wants an extra set of quotes, or someting all lower case, when the lesson has it capitalized, etc. Not a show stopper, but sometimes annoying.

Codeanywhere

  • Current Opinion: I still like it. Has a few issues with one of the older browsers I access, but not holding that against them.
  • Value: For my situation, I still think its a good deal. I access from a number of PCs, tablets and even my phone.
  • Competition: To be honest, I haven't looked.
  • Major Pros: Accessibility.
  • Major Cons: I struggle with keeping indentation correct. It tries to help... but for me, usually ends up making it worse. I've turned all of the auto functions off, and its better.

LinkedIn Learning

  • Current Opinion: Excellent content and instructors. I do like codecademy's in browser follow along better.
  • Value: I plan to keep it after the trial, on a monthly basis. I figure I have a couple months worth of use still in it, but not a whole year.
  • Competition: The only competitor I've looked at is CodecademyPro, above. There are local bootcamps that host free workshops about once a month, but I haven't gone to any yet.
  • Major Pros: Video content can be downloaded for later(still have to be a paying member to play them). Quality instruction. Mostly relevant. I did catch a few references to things that have changed, but they were in an overview module, not a specific language.
  • Major Cons: The supplemental files are great if you always work from one computer. This is not the case for me, but I still like the video content.

GitHub

  • Current Opinion: I'll be honest, I struggled to understand how to use it for a day or two. But, I now have this project backed up there, and split out monthly branches for historical views.
  • Value: Great for a free resource. I'd recommend to anyone working a project.
  • Competition: I haven't looked into any.
  • Major Pros: Version control. And community support.
  • Major Cons: A little tough to grasp their lingo and functionality. I understood the concept just fine, but the execution was a little challenging at first.
